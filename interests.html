Interests


Apart from being a student, One of my favorite hobbies is breeding aquatic life, specifically Neocaridina shrimp and betta fish. With Neocaridina shrimp, I’m fascinated by their vibrant colors, like reds, blues, and oranges, and how selective breeding can enhance those traits over generations. I maintain separate tanks with precise water parameters like pH, temperature, and TDS (total dissolved solids) to ensure they thrive. Watching a colony of shrimp multiply and grow is incredibly rewarding, especially when I spot rare color morphs emerging.

When it comes to betta fish, I enjoy working with both their beauty and their personalities. Breeding them involves carefully selecting pairs for their fin patterns, colors, and health. I condition the fish before breeding with live foods, then use a breeding tank setup where the male builds a bubble nest for the eggs. It’s amazing to watch the entire process, from courtship to the moment the fry hatch and start swimming freely.

This hobby combines my love for creativity and science, as I get to experiment with genetics while creating visually stunning tanks. Plus, the upkeep—water changes, aquascaping, and feeding—feels like a calming routine that balances my day.
